Macroecology, plant systematics, and conservation under global change

My research focuses on large-scale patterns of plant diversity, the drivers of species richness, and translating ecological knowledge into conservation planning under climate and human footprint change.

About

Dr. Nawal Shrestha (PhD, Institute of Botany, Chinese Academy of Sciences, 2015) is an Assistant Professor at Kathmandu University. He has held postdoctoral positions at Peking University and research positions at Harvard University and Lanzhou University. His work blends phylogenetics, macroecology, and spatial conservation prioritisation.

Selected roles: Researcher (Harvard University), Young Researcher (Lanzhou University), Postdoc (Peking University).

Research interests

  • Plant systematics and macroevolution
  • Macroecology of plant diversity and drivers of species richness
  • Conservation planning under climate & human footprint change
  • Species distribution modelling and vulnerability assessment
